# AI Hairstyle Changer FAQ

## How does an AI hairstyle changer work?

It uses an uploaded portrait as a reference and follows the selected hairstyle preset plus the editable prompt to change the visible hair. The prompt asks the editor to preserve the same person, face, clothing, pose, and background.

## What photo works best for changing hairstyles?

Use a clear front-facing or slight three-quarter portrait with the full head visible, even lighting, a readable hairline, and minimal obstruction around the face.

## Can users try both women's and men's hairstyles?

Yes. The page includes long layers, waves, bobs, short crops, fades, curls, slicked-back styling, and buzz cuts.

## Can a hairstyle preset be customized?

Yes. Every preset fills the prompt, which remains editable for length, fringe, parting, curl definition, volume, texture, and preservation instructions.

## Will the result keep the same face?

The prompt requests identity preservation, but generated results can vary. Users should review each result and refine the prompt if the face or background changes more than expected.

## Is this an exact haircut simulation?

No. It is a visual concept. A real haircut depends on hair texture, density, growth pattern, current length, maintenance, and professional technique.

## Is Toolaze AI Hairstyle Changer free with no signup?

Yes. The standard page experience is positioned as free, with no signup and no watermark.

## Can users download the hairstyle preview?

Yes. The shared image-generation workspace provides save and download controls after a result is generated.

## Can the preview be used as a salon reference?

Yes. It can communicate a general direction, but a stylist should confirm what is realistic for the user's actual hair.
